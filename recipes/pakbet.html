Pakbet

Description

Pakbet short for Pinakbet, is a traditional Filipino vegetable stew originating from the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It's known for its unique flavor derived from shrimp paste (bagoong) and the use of various local vegetables. The name "Pinakbet" is derived from the Ilocano word "pinakebbet," meaning "shrunk" or "shriveled," referring to how the vegetables are cooked.

Ingredients

  • Eggplant
  • Okra
  • Ampalaya (Bitter melon)
  • Squash (Kalabasa)
  • Long beans (Sitaw)
  • Pork
  • Shrimp paste(Bagoong)
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Fry pork belly in hot oil until brown and crispy. Drain and set aside.
  2. Using the same pan, saute onions, ginger, garlic. Add tomatoes and continue saut√©ing until soft. Then Add shrimp paste. Mix well.
  3. Add squash, water and Knorr Shrimp Cube. Cover and let cook over medium heat for 8 minutes.
  4. Add sitaw, ampalaya, eggplants and okra. Mix and cover. Let cook for another 5-8 minutes. Serve warm.
