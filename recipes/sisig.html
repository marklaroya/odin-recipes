Sisig

Description

The word "sisig" is believed to come from the old Tagalog word "sisigan," which means "to make sour".
Traditional sisig is made with pork face and ears and chicken liver that's boiled, grilled, then cooked with aromatics.

Ingredients

  • Pork (Belly or ears)
  • Onions
  • Chillies
  • Seasonings (SoySauce,Vinegar,Salt,Calamansi,Garlic and Bay leaves)
  • Egg
  • Mayonnaise
  • Chopped Shallots
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Sizzling Pork Sisig | In Defense of OffalCombine pork belly, face, ears, vinegar, soy sauce in a stock pot.Fill with water until meat is covered. Add bay leaves, salt. Bring to a boil then simmer for at least an hour or until meat is no longer tight and tough.
  2. Remove meat from pot and grill for 10 minutes.
  3. Chop pork belly and face meat into 1/4 - 1/2 inch cubes. Finely chop pig ear pieces.
  4. Mix chopped pork in a bowl and add Thai chilis, kalamansi juice, vinegar, salt, pepper, and garlic powder (or sisig seasoning packet)
  5. In a saute pan over medium heat, add 1 T oil and saute shallots for 1-2 minutes. Add chicken livers; mash and saute until cooked through.
  6. Add chopped pork (with liquids) to saute pan, saute until meat is hot. Add mayonnaise and combine.
  7. (Optional) Heat a cast iron platter, add 1 T butter, add a serving of sisig and top with a cracked egg while still hot.
